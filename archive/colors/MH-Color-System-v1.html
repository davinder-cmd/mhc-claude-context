Section 3
Token Architecture
Three-tier system: Global (raw values) → Semantic (intent) → Component (scoped). Semantic tokens are the primary interface for design and development.

Token naming convention

Each tier resolves to the one below it. Change a global token and everything updates. Change a semantic token and only intent-matched components update.

Section 7
Illustration & Imagery Direction
Moving from the current "vibrant corporate" illustration style toward something softer, more editorial, and more human. Flat with subtle depth — not the gear-and-mountain tech aesthetic.

Illustration style rules

Style: Flat with subtle dimensionality (soft shadows, not gradients). Think Noom editorial meets Apple Health simplicity. No heavy textures, no 3D renders.

Max 4 colors per illustration: 1 from the primary/secondary palette, 1 from tertiaries, 1–2 from the neutral ramp. Background tints from the 50-level surfaces.

People: Simplified, warm, inclusive. Not hyper-realistic, not stick figures — somewhere in between. Use the avatar palette for skin tones and clothing. People should feel like they belong in the app, not pasted from stock.

Scenes: Abstract where possible. A circle + gradient suggesting sunrise. A wavy line suggesting breathing. Let the user's imagination fill the gaps.

Empty states: Single-hue illustrations using the 100-300 range of the relevant palette. Muted, gentle, encouraging — not a dead-end. Always paired with an action.

Section 8
Corporate Brand Bridge
How every app color traces back to the corporate palette. The app is a sibling — clearly from the same family, but with its own personality suited to the consumer-facing context.
Corporate colorApp evolutionRelationship
#0F497F Brand Blue → #2E6DA4 Ocean Blue Same hue (209°), +12% lighter, warmer. Anchor evolved.
#04A0B7 Aqua Blue → #48B5A0 Sea Glass Shifted from cyan toward green-teal. Warmer, more organic.
#F15922 Lava Orange → #E8985A Warm Apricot Desaturated, shifted warmer. From "alert" to "warm invitation."
#52A045 Spring Green → #7AA872 Sage Desaturated, more muted. From "billboard" to "garden."
#062A42 Night Sky → #153352 Deep Water Slightly lighter, same family. Used for headings, not backgrounds.
#6E7A7D Slate → #5A6878 Neutral 500 Blue-tinted instead of green-gray. Cohesive with primary.
(new) + #9189BD Soft Lavender New tertiary for mental health / sleep / mindfulness content.
