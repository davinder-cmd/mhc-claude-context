v2 → v3 changes

What's different and why

v2 had the right structure but the wrong temperature and too little contrast in the places that matter most. Here's what moved.

What wasn't working

Blue-tinted neutrals — Cool grays (#F5F8FB, #E6ECF2) made the app feel clinical and corporate. Every screen had a faint blue wash that read "enterprise dashboard."

Under-saturated domains — Teal #48B5A0, Apricot #E8985A, Lavender #9189BD were all playing it safe. Category indicators whispered when they needed to speak. Compare to Withings' bold green/purple/red pills.

Timid data viz — Sparklines used the lightest end of each ramp. Data — the thing people actually come to see — had the least visual weight on screen.

What v3 fixes

Warm-taupe neutral ramp — #FAF8F5 replaces #F5F8FB. The entire app shifts from "hospital corridor" to "warm linen." This is the single biggest change — it transforms how every other color reads on top of it.

Boosted domain saturation — Every domain color pushed 15-20% more saturated. Teal #35B89E, Sage #6BAD5E, Lavender #8B7FC0. Category pills are now confident, like Withings'.

Data gets the loudest color — Charts and progress bars use bold, punchy fills. Data typography is large and high-contrast. Like both reference apps: quiet structure, loud data.

Usage system

Where each color earns its place

Same domain-coding logic as v2, but with boosted saturation and warm neutral foundation.
Ocean Blue — Structure & Action

Header gradients, primary CTA buttons, active navigation, links, focus rings. The spine of every screen. Stays strong and confident.

Teal — Activity & Vitality

Steps, workouts, fitness goals, movement data, wellness scores. The "body in motion" color. Used boldly in progress bars and data numbers.

Apricot — Rewards & Warmth

Points, streaks, badges, achievements, premium features. The warm "payoff" color. Used on tinted card surfaces and bold accent numbers.

Lavender — Mind & Rest

Sleep, mental health, therapy, mindfulness, AI insights, emotional wellbeing. The introspective color. Tinted surfaces and data numbers.

Sage — Nutrition & Prevention

Diet, recipes, meal plans, preventive care, wellness programs. The "tending your garden" color. Category pills and chart segments.

Warm Neutrals — Canvas

App backgrounds (#FAF8F5), card surfaces (white), body text, borders, dividers. The warm linen canvas that makes everything else pop.

The 60-30-10 rule (revised for v3)

60%
Warm neutrals + surfaces
Cream backgrounds, white cards, warm-taupe text and borders. The breathable, human canvas.
30%
Primary blue
Header, CTA buttons, active states, links. Structural color that anchors every screen.
10%
Domain accents — bold and committed
Category pills, data numbers, chart fills, tinted card surfaces. Small area, high saturation. This is where character lives.
