Blue as Mood — Light + Atmospheric

Still light, still warm. But the blue now sets a mood in the header zone — a gradient that breathes, that shifts from deep to luminous, that makes you feel something before you read anything. Inspired by Calm's atmospheric color, adapted for a light, multi-domain health app.

The shift

Color as mood, not just function

In v3, blue was a button color and a header background. Here, blue is an atmosphere in the hero zone — a luminous gradient that transitions from deep to light, creating a sense of sky and calm. Below the fold, the warm-taupe canvas takes over for data. The result: emotional resonance at the top, readability at the bottom.

Gradient anatomy

How the mood gradient works

The key trick: the gradient doesn't stop at an edge — it dissolves smoothly into the warm-taupe canvas. No hard line. The blue atmosphere just evaporates as you scroll into content territory.

B. Cerulean — "Morning Sky" gradient

0% — Deep ocean · #073E64
25% — Cerulean · #0B6BA6
50% — Sky · #3A96CC
72% — Mist · #8EC4E6
88% — Haze · #D2E8F5
100% — Warm canvas · #FAF8F5
Feels like looking at the horizon over water at 7am. Warm, hopeful, energetic. The hue stays consistent (205°) as it desaturates and lightens toward the canvas.

C. Cobalt — "Deep Calm" gradient

0% — Midnight · #0D1F45
20% — Deep cobalt · #1A3878
42% — Electric · #2B5AAE
62% — Periwinkle · #6B94CF
78% — Steel mist · #A8C5E6
92% — Faint haze · #DDE9F4
Feels like deep water — rich, contemplative, premium. The indigo shift (218°) gives it the Calm-like quality. More gravitas, more distinction, more mood.
Scroll behavior

On scroll, the header compresses from the full atmospheric gradient to a compact colored bar. The mood shrinks but doesn't disappear — you still feel the blue presence even in a collapsed nav.

Across screens

The mood adapts to context

Not every screen gets the same gradient depth. The home screen has the fullest expression. Secondary screens get a shorter, quieter version. Detail screens may use just a hint. The mood is a spectrum, not a switch.
