Recommendation

The case for each

B. Cerulean Punch

Choose B if you want warmth to be the whole story. The warmer hue (205°) sings on the warm-taupe canvas. The blue+teal pair feels like they grew up together — coastal, health-forward, optimistic. This is the Headspace/Calm lane: approachable, energetic, human.

Best for: consumer-first health apps where warmth and approachability are the primary brand attributes. People use this because it feels good, not because their employer told them to.

C. Electric Cobalt

Choose C if you want a point of view that's harder to copy. The cooler hue (218°) creates deliberate tension with the warm canvas — and that tension is what makes it memorable. The blue is clearly different from the teal, which strengthens domain coding. This is the Linear/Revolut lane: premium, intentional, distinctive.

Best for: enterprise health platforms where credibility and sophistication matter alongside warmth. People trust this because it looks like it was designed by people who care about details.

Both pass WCAG AA across all pairings. Both work in light and dark mode. The question isn't accessibility or function — it's personality.
