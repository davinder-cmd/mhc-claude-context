Panel notes — Direction A

  • Teal carries natural health connotations (water, life) without being clinical
  • Warm apricot accent creates complementary tension — prevents feeling "cold"
  • Deep Forest (#0F3D3A) replaces Night Sky as the dark anchor — warmer, less corporate
  • Hinge Health uses a similar green-family commitment but theirs is more saturated — this is softer
  • Differentiates clearly from Personify Health's mustard-teal and Thrive's dark teal
  • The neutral ramp is teal-tinted — everything feels cohesive, not assembled from parts
  • Risk: Could feel too "wellness spa" if not grounded with strong typography and structure
Primary on white: 5.2:1 ✓ AA Interactive on white: 3.4:1 ✓ AA Large Deep on surface: 12.8:1 ✓ AAA

Positioning

Emotional register

Grounded, natural, trustworthy but warm. "Your health is in good hands." Less corporate authority, more wise companion.

Competitive space

Sits between Withings' minimalism and Noom's warmth. Distinct from Personify's mustard warmth. Doesn't overlap Thrive's dark teal.

Brand bridge

Directly evolves your existing Aqua Blue — stakeholders will see the lineage. The teal family is already in your DNA.

Panel notes — Direction B

  • Indigo/violet is increasingly associated with wellness, mindfulness, and mental health
  • No major digital health competitor owns this color space — high differentiation
  • Coral accent is warm and inviting — signals care and humanity
  • Dark mode is particularly beautiful — the purple-dark surfaces feel premium
  • Biggest departure from current brand — may face internal resistance
  • Calm, Headspace, and some therapy apps use adjacent purples — could signal "mental health only" if not careful
  • Risk: Furthest from existing brand. Stakeholder buy-in could be challenging. May read as "meditation app" rather than full-spectrum health platform.
Primary on white: 6.8:1 ✓ AAA Interactive on white: 3.6:1 ✓ AA Large Deep on surface: 14.2:1 ✓ AAA

Positioning

Emotional register

Contemporary, emotionally aware, sophisticated. "We see you as a whole person." Signals that this isn't just another benefits portal.

Competitive space

Wide open. No direct competitor in digital health owns indigo. Clear differentiation from Personify's warm gold and Thrive's teal.

Brand bridge

Weakest link to current brand. Would require the strongest internal narrative about why the app deserves its own identity.

Panel notes — Direction C

  • Safest evolution — clearly "Mobile Health" but refined, warmer, more human
  • Ocean Blue (#2E6DA4) is lighter and warmer than Brand Blue (#0F497F) — less corporate, more approachable
  • Sea Glass accent bridges to your existing Aqua Blue heritage but is desaturated and warmer
  • Apple Health, One Medical, and Forward all use adjacent blues — you'd be in premium health company
  • Easiest stakeholder sell — "we evolved it" not "we replaced it"
  • The blue-teal combination is the most conventional digital health pairing
  • Risk: May not feel like enough of a change. Could still read as "another blue health app." Lowest differentiation of the three options.
Primary on white: 4.8:1 ✓ AA Interactive on white: 3.2:1 ✓ AA Large Deep on surface: 11.4:1 ✓ AAA

Positioning

Emotional register

Reliable, clean, professional but warm. "We're the health platform you already trust." Evolutionary, not revolutionary.

Competitive space

Sits alongside Apple Health and One Medical — premium but familiar. Risk of blending in with other blue health apps.

Brand bridge

Strongest connection to existing brand. Stakeholders will immediately recognize the evolution. Lowest internal friction.
