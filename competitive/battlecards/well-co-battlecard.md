## Landmine Questions

Ask prospects to ask Well.co:

1. **"What specific clinical programs do you offer for diabetes, MSK, or behavioral health?"**
   *Exposes: May lack clinical depth*

2. **"Your guarantee is 1:1 - why not higher? What's included in that calculation?"**
   *Exposes: Lower confidence vs. our 3:1*

3. **"Can you provide references from employers our size in our industry?"**
   *Exposes: May have limited proof points*

4. **"If we need specialized DTx programs, do you have them or do we need additional vendors?"**
   *Exposes: Potential gaps requiring point solutions*

5. **"How many years have you been in business? How many members do you serve?"**
   *Exposes: Newer entrant, less scale*

## Objection Handling

### "They also have an ROI guarantee"
> **Response:** "We both believe in guaranteeing results - that's great. But look at the guarantee: they promise 1:1, we promise 3:1 or 30% of fees returned. Our confidence in our clinical outcomes is literally three times theirs."

### "They have human coaches - Well Guides"
> **Response:** "Human touch matters, and we agree. But coaching alone doesn't treat diabetes or chronic back pain. Our Digital Care Paths combine clinical protocols with behavior change science. We address conditions, not just provide guidance."

### "Their engagement metrics are impressive - 25% daily active users"
> **Response:** "Engagement matters, but engagement doing what? 300 interactions per year is meaningless if they're not addressing the conditions driving your healthcare costs. Our DCPs engage members in clinically rigorous programs that deliver measurable outcomes."

### "They seem more innovative and modern"
> **Response:** "Innovation for its own sake isn't valuable - outcomes are. We've been delivering clinical programs that reduce healthcare costs with a guaranteed ROI. New doesn't equal better when your employees' health is at stake."
