MHC Inside Alight

3 schemes designed to differentiate MHC within the Alight Worklife shell

Design Problem
Alight Worklife uses cornflower blue, light gray backgrounds, green progress bars, and pink accents. MHC's experience lives inside this container for partner-distributed members. The risk: MHC blends into Alight's chrome and loses its identity. Each scheme below creates a clear visual break — the member should feel they've entered a distinct health experience, not another tab.

Avoid: Cornflower blue (too close to Alight's primary), cool gray backgrounds (merge with Alight's shell), bright green (Alight's progress bar).
Scheme 01
Aqua Warmth

Recommended — best differentiation

MHC's Aqua Blue is teal — visually distinct from Alight's cornflower blue. The warm sand background immediately signals "you've left Alight's space." Mango rewards pop against the teal in a way Alight's green progress bar can't. This is the clearest identity break with the lowest brand risk.

Aqua ≠ Alight blue. Alight uses cornflower (#4078d4). MHC's Aqua (#04a0b7) is teal — completely different hue family. No visual confusion.
Warm background is the instant break. Alight is cool gray (#f0f0f0). The moment a member taps into MHC, the sand background signals a different space.

Scheme 02
Night Sky + Tangerine

Maximum contrast. Night Sky header creates an unmistakable visual break from Alight's light chrome. Tangerine as the signature accent is bold, warm, and completely absent from Alight's palette. This says "you've entered a premium health experience" the moment you land. The Lava Orange adds energy to CTAs without competing with Tangerine's role on engagement.

Scheme 03
Brand Blue + Living Green

Leans into health. Brand Blue header for trust, Spring Green and Lime as the dominant accents. Alight uses green sparingly (progress bar only) — MHC can own it as a full identity layer. This says "health and vitality" in a way Alight's benefits-focused UI doesn't attempt. Risk: the Brand Blue header is closest to Alight's blue of any scheme, so the green has to do more differentiation work.
