MHC Inside Allstate Good Life

3 schemes designed to differentiate MHC within the Allstate partner shell

Design Problem
Allstate Good Life uses indigo/purple headers, warm cream/peach coach cards, white backgrounds, and photo-heavy content. The UI is airy, warm, and consumer-friendly — which is close to where MHC wants to be. The challenge here is more subtle than Alight: Allstate already feels wellness-y, so MHC needs to carve out a distinct "clinical health" identity without looking generic.

Avoid: Indigo/purple (Allstate's identity), pale cream (their coach cards), white-only backgrounds (merge with their shell), blue-purple links (their CTA color).
Scheme 01
Aqua Teal + Warm Sand

Recommended — clearest identity break

Teal is the sharpest differentiator from Allstate's purple-blue world. The warm sand background is distinctly not-white, creating an immediate context shift. Mango for rewards avoids Allstate's cream-yellow while feeling warmer. This is the safest bet for SDK embeds where MHC needs to clearly be "the health experience" inside Allstate's benefits wrapper.

Scheme 02
Night Sky + Lava

The bold play. Allstate Good Life is entirely light and airy — a Night Sky header is the hardest possible break. Lava Orange as the signature accent has zero overlap with Allstate's palette. This reads as a distinct premium health app that happens to be accessible from within Allstate. The strongest "you're in a different place now" signal.

Scheme 03
Spring Green Vitality

Nature-forward health. Allstate's "Wellbeing Assistants" section uses cream/peach — no green at all. MHC can own green as the health vitality signal. Brand Blue header gives familiar trust; Spring Green and Lime do the differentiation work in the body. This is the "health lives here" play — Allstate handles benefits, MHC is where you get healthier.
