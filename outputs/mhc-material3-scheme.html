How M3 Color Works
Material Design 3 generates a full scheme from 3 key colors: Primary, Secondary, and Tertiary. Each key color produces a tonal palette (13 tones from 0–100). Light theme roles pull from specific tones: Primary = tone 40, Container = tone 90, On-Container = tone 10, etc. This creates automatic contrast ratios and a complete system from just 3 inputs.

MHC key colors used:
Primary → Brand Blue #0f497f (trust, authority, clinical)
Secondary → Aqua Blue #04a0b7 (engagement, interactive)
Tertiary → Mango #f3b31e (rewards, warmth, achievement)

Notes
What M3 Gets You

Systematic contrast. Every On-color is guaranteed to meet WCAG AA against its background. No manual checking. Primary (P-40) on On-Primary (P-100) = 7.3:1 ratio.

Dark mode for free. Flip the tone mappings: Primary goes from P-40 → P-80, containers from tone 90 → tone 30. The whole system inverts without redesigning anything.

Surface hierarchy without shadows. M3's surface containers (N-96 through N-90) create depth through color steps, not box-shadow. Cleaner rendering, especially on lower-end Android devices in your member base.

Partner theming. The three key colors are the only inputs. For white-label SDK distributions (Alight, Allstate), you can swap key colors and the entire system regenerates. One system, multiple brand expressions.

Android-native alignment. MHC's Android app is Java/native. M3 is the Android design system. Adopting it means your design tokens map 1:1 to what Android engineers expect. Less translation, fewer bugs.
