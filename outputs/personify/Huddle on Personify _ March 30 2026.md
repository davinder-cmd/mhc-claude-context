# **Huddle on Personify**

Mon, 30 Mar 26 · ellen@mobilehealthconsumer.com, Stacey Anger

### **Personify App Demo Overview**

* Stacey demonstrated Personify health app on her phone  
* No AI features visible in her version despite marketing claims  
* Recording declined due to husband’s workplace connection concerns  
* App performance notably faster than current MobileHealthConsumer platform  
  * No loading screens observed  
  * Instant page transitions throughout demo

### **DL Key Take Aways:**

* Performance: Their app is very, very fast. Only once did it take a few seconds for a page to load and it showed "we're gathering your stuff" message. (Note: we couldn't get the message to show again for a screenshot; it must have cached something and loaded faster the next time)  
* The app is VERY dynamic  
  * Cards flip over to show more info in many places throughout the app  
  * Celebration modals for accomplishments and badges  
  * Text boxes open/close to show/hide more info  
* Home page is busy, but still feels simple  
* UI Components are very standardized \- looks like they're "out of the box"/ native components.

### **Home Screen & Navigation Structure**

* Clean interface with rewards, announcements, daily cards prominently featured  
* Stats section connects to Apple Health with extensive device integration options  
* Daily cards provide simple engagement tasks (5 points each)  
* Native iOS components used throughout rather than custom UI elements  
* Bottom navigation includes: Home, Health, Social, More

### **Tracker Data & Goal Setting**

* Apple Health integration with comprehensive device support  
* Manual entry options with filtering by source (self-entered vs device)  
* 30,000 daily step limit (vs MobileHealthConsumer’s 40,000 default)  
* Multiple goal levels for different step targets  
* Sleep tracking with hours-based input rather than sleep/wake times

### **Rewards & Challenges System**

* Two-tier action system: curated shortlist \+ comprehensive “how to earn more points”  
* Team challenges mirror MobileHealthConsumer’s v3 functionality  
* Voucher redemption feature allows managers to award points directly  
* Gift card store opens in web browser within app  
* Trophy case uses standard iOS badge paradigm without categorization

### **Health Assessment & Programs**

* NCQA certified health assessment (one question per screen with progress bar)  
* Risk stratification by category rather than program recommendations  
* Journey content organized by categories with multiple paths exposed  
* Coaching requires phone scheduling, no text-based interaction  
* Limited AI integration despite marketing positioning

### **Technical Integration Challenges**

* Same Apple Health sync issues as MobileHealthConsumer  
* FAQ section addresses identical troubleshooting steps  
* Direct device connections for select trackers (Fitbit, etc.)  
* Support includes AI chat feature for customer service
