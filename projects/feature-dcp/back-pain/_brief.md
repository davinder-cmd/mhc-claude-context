## What This Sub-Feature Is

The Back Pain DCP is MHC's flagship MSK (musculoskeletal) program. It guides members through evidence-based physical therapy exercises for low back pain — delivered via video, guided movement, and AI coaching. It is the highest-volume MSK program and a direct competitive response to Hinge Health and Sword Health.

## Member Goal

Reduce pain, regain mobility, and build confidence that they can manage their back through exercise — without needing surgery or ongoing clinic visits.

## Business Goal

Deliver measurable outcomes on pain scores and functional improvement. MSK is one of the highest-cost conditions for employers — MHC's ROI claim depends heavily on MSK DCP outcomes. Back Pain is the most common entry point into the MSK suite.

## Clinical Framework

- Evidence base: Physical therapy, exercise-based rehabilitation, pain neuroscience education
- Intake/progress assessment: Pain scale (0–10), functional movement assessment
- AI coaching methodology: Motivational Interviewing, with exercise-specific guidance
- AI coach domain: MSK agent (separate from Behavioral Health agent)

## Key Constraints

- Exercise content is video-based — AI coach supports but does not replace the exercise library
- Pain neuroscience education must be handled carefully — avoid catastrophizing language
- Must accommodate varying pain levels and physical limitations — never assume full mobility
- MSK AI agent is distinct from Behavioral Health and General agents — do not conflate
- No cross-session memory yet — each coaching conversation starts fresh

## Differentiators vs. Competitors

- Hinge Health: direct competitor, well-funded, strong brand in MSK — our primary threat
- Sword Health: also strong in MSK + behavioral health combo
- Key differentiator question: how does MHC MSK stand out beyond the program content itself?
- Potential angles: integration with broader wellness platform, employer ROI story, biometric data feed
