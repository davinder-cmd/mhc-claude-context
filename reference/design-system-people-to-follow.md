# Design System Leaders — Reference

People worth following for design systems thinking. Not an exhaustive list — these are the voices that consistently produce practical, applicable insights.

---

## Tier 1: Foundational Thinkers

These shaped how the industry thinks about design systems.

### Brad Frost
**Known for:** Atomic Design methodology (Atoms → Molecules → Organisms → Templates → Pages)

**Why he matters:** Your Foundation → Atoms → Components hierarchy is derived from his work. He defined the vocabulary.

**Best content:**
- *Atomic Design* (book) — free at atomicdesign.bradfrost.com
- "Design Systems Are for User Interfaces" — pushback on over-scoping systems

**Follow:** bradfrost.com, @brad_frost

**What to learn:** Component hierarchy, when a system is "done enough," avoiding perfectionism

---

### Nathan Curtis
**Known for:** EightShapes; most rigorous design systems consulting practice

**Why he matters:** Best thinking on governance, documentation structure, and how systems scale. His articles are the closest thing to a design systems MBA.

**Best content:**
- "Documenting Components" — how to structure component docs
- "Tokens in Design Systems" — token architecture
- "Design System Tiers" — how to layer a system for different maturity levels
- All articles at medium.com/eightshapes-llc

**Follow:** eightshapes.com, @nathanacurtis

**What to learn:** Documentation rigor, governance, when to say no to requests

---

### Jina Anne
**Known for:** Design Tokens (coined the term), Clarity conference, formerly Salesforce Lightning

**Why she matters:** If you use tokens, you're using her framework. She defined how design and engineering share a common language.

**Best content:**
- "Design Tokens for Dummies" — foundational talk
- Clarity conference talks (clarityconf.com)
- Design Tokens W3C Community Group work

**Follow:** @jina, sushiandrobots.com

**What to learn:** Token architecture, naming conventions, bridging design-engineering gap

---

## Tier 2: Practitioners at Scale

These run or have run design systems at major companies. They've solved problems at scale.

### Diana Mounter
**Known for:** GitHub Primer design system

**Why she matters:** Primer is one of the best-documented systems. She's written extensively on accessibility and system governance.

**Best content:**
- "Design Systems at GitHub" — how a small team runs a system for a large product
- Primer documentation (primer.style) — study the structure itself

**Follow:** @broccolini

**What to learn:** Lean team governance, accessibility integration, documentation clarity

---

### Dan Mall
**Known for:** SuperFriendly; design systems strategy consulting; "Hot Potato" process

**Why he matters:** Focuses on how teams adopt systems, not just how to build them. Strong on the human/organizational side.

**Best content:**
- *Design That Scales* (book) — systems for organizations
- "Selling Design Systems" — how to get buy-in
- "Design System Pilots" — how to start small

**Follow:** danmall.com, @danmall

**What to learn:** Adoption strategy, stakeholder buy-in, starting small

---

### Amy Hupe
**Known for:** GOV.UK Design System, content strategy for design systems

**Why she matters:** Best thinking on how to write component documentation, naming conventions, and content guidelines. If your docs are confusing, read her work.

**Best content:**
- "How to write useful component documentation"
- "The myth of the universal design system"
- amyhupe.co.uk/articles

**Follow:** @Amy_Hupe, amyhupe.co.uk

## How to Use This Reference

### When validating a decision:
1. Check if Nathan Curtis or Amy Hupe has written about it
2. Look at how Material/Carbon/Polaris solved it
3. Post in Design Systems Slack if you're stuck

### When building documentation:
1. Study Carbon's component doc structure
2. Read Amy Hupe on writing component docs
3. Compare your draft against Primer's style

### When stuck on governance:
1. Nathan Curtis articles on EightShapes
2. Dan Mall on adoption and buy-in
3. Diana Mounter on lean team governance

### When facing accessibility questions:
1. Sara Soueidan for implementation details
2. Atlassian/Carbon for system-level patterns
3. GOV.UK for real-world government-scale accessibility
